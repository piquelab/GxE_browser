Allele-specific Expression

  • Data Tables

Gene-environment interactions (GxE) are known to play an important role in common disease risk and biomedically-relevant traits. However, quantifying the extent to which the environment can modulate genetic effects on human quantitative phenotypes is challenging and remains largely undefined. We have investigated the transcriptional response to 50 treatments in 5 different cell types and 15 individuals (for a total of 960 RNA-seq samples). We discovered 1,530 unique genes with allele specific expression (ASE) (FDR<10%) across 73 cell-type x treatment combinations with strongest gene response. While the majority of ASE is consistent between treatment and control, we also find 260 instances of GxE spanning 219 unique genes and 35 treatments. Stronger GxE is present for genes with larger differential expression. 47% (103 out of 219) of genes that contain either evidence of GxE are identified by GWAS as being associated with various complex traits (p < 2.2x10^-16, OR = 3.3 over DE genes). Through specific examples we show that GxE detected in vitro can inform the genetic mechanisms underlying variation in complex traits.
